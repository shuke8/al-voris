Tour Description

Travelling in train through Uzbekistan will be the fastest and the safest way , there are three types of trains in Uzbekistan Afrasiyab Fast Train , Sharq Express Train and normal train – slow train, railways connectes the former silk road cities in the country maybe following the silk road by trains, in every city you will see the ancient monuments minarets and modern railway stations, by this tour you will cross the Kizilkum desert by train rather than sitting in the bus or taking flight during long journeys by train you will find more local friends and you will feel yourself comfortable and you will have good atmosphere and condition for reading your lovely book around the bullet ways.

Routes

  • Day 1 : Tashkent

    Arrival at Tashkent international airport.

  • Day 2 : Tashkent - Fergana - Margilan - Fergana - (319,2km - 5hrs 6min)

    Early breakfast in hotel and check out

    Train to Fergana valley via Kamchiq Tunnel You will see the biggest railway tunnel in Central Asia. The Kamchiq Tunnel is a 19.2 kilometres (11.9 mi) long railway tunnel in Uzbekistan, which makes it the longest tunnel in Central Asia.

    Construction began September 2013 and the excavation works were finished 27 February 2016Margilan city

    Lunch in local restaurant

    City tour:Margilan - is one of the most ancient cities of Uzbekistan. It is situated in Fergana Valley on the intersection of ancient trade routes. The city was well known already in antiquity because it made the best silk in Central Asia, able to compete in quality and beauty with that of Chinese one. Caravans with Margilan silk tailed to Kashgar, Bagdad, Khorasan and even to Greece moving along the Silk Road. Two silk factories were set up in Margilan. One of them is the largest inter price producing “Khan-Atlas” in Central Asia. The factory produces daily up to 2.5 km of fabric, which is in demand world-wide.

    Dinner in local restaurant

  • Day 3 : Fergana Valley - Rishtan - Kokand - (95,6km)

    Early breakfast in hotel and check out

    Transfer to Rishtan

    City tour :

    Halfway between Kokand and Fergana is the town of Rishtan, famed for its bright blue and green ceramics, with the unique “Ishkor” glaze. Legend claims the art is over 800 years old, passed down from father to son, using local red clay and natural pigments made from minerals and mountain grasses.

    Lunch in local restaurant

    Transfer to Kokand

    City tour :

    Khudayar-Khan Fortress-each khanates of the Turan had its own grand citadel. One of these monuments is a Citadel (Urda) that was constructed by the order of last Kokand ruler Khudayarkhan in 1872. According to the historical sources, this citadel of Khudayarkhan (1831-1882) was built on the ruins of the citadel of Muhammad Ali Khan. Best masters of the Kokand and Bukhara were invited for the construction of the fortress. In accordance of the project architect Mir Ubaydillah the building was erected on the hill above the high brick foundation.

    Facing and decorations were executed by the most well-known masters and calligraphers of those times.

    Dinner in local restaurant

    Overnight in Kokand

    Sitorai Mohi Khosa ( the former summer residence of the last emirate of Bukhara you can see here European and Asian mixed architecture )

  • Day 8 : Samarkand

    Breakfast in hotel

    City tour :

    Registan Square - is a real gem located in the very heart of the ancient city of Samarkand. It has gained its worldwide fame thanks to the great architectural ensemble that become a monument of the oriental architecture. There are three madrassahs on the Square: Ulugbek Madrasah, SherDor Madrasah, Tillya-Kori Madrasah, that are the main sights of the city. They were erected by two rulers at different times

    Bibi - Khanum Mosque( One of the biggest mosque in Central Asia, it was erected according to the order of Tamerlane after his victorious Indian campaign in 1399.)

    Lunch time

    Ulughbek observatory – which is founded and ruled by Tamerlan’s grandson Mirzo Ulughbek. Observatory was unique construction for its time. The basis of observatory was giant goniometer (vertical circle), radius of circle was equal 40,212 meters, and length of arc was 63 meters. The main instrument-sextant was oriented with amazing exactness by line of meridian from south to north.

    Shakhi - Zinda - is the burial place of royal persons and nobles. But the main mausoleum from which the necropolis starts seems to be the imaginary grave of Prophet Muhammad’s cousin, Kusam Ibn Abbas. The complex was called “Shakhi Zinda” that means in Persian “The Alive King”.

    Dineer in local restaurant

    Overnight in Samarkand

  • Day 9 : Samarkand - Shakhrisabz - (84,2km / 1hr 45min)

    Breakfast in hotel

    Transfer to the station

    Train to Shakhrisabz-(10:16 - 12:25 / 84,2km)

    City tour :

    “AK-SARAY” Palace - the architectural décor, featuring a wide variety of designs and colors, is particularly noteworthy in the artistic appearance of Ak-saray. The use of brick mosaic work, mainly dark and light blue in color, forming large geometrical and epigraphic designs on a background of polished brick, gives the portal a special softness of color and an air of grand mystery.

    KOK-GUMBAZ MOSQUE - the building of the mosque is crowned by a huge dome covered with blue ceramic tiles. It gave the name to the mosque- KOK GUMBAZ that means “Blue Dome”. Around the tholobate of the dome there is the inscription with white glazed tiles: “Sovereignty belongs to Allah, wealth belongs to Allah”.

    THE DOR-UT TILOVAT - memorial complex was formed after the death in 1370-1371 of the eminent religious leader Shamsiddin Kulal, the founder of Sufism, the spiritual mentir of Emir Taragay and of Timur himself, and the teacher of Bahauddin Naqshbandi.

    Transfer to the station

    Train to Samarkand - (15:43 - 17:52 / 84,2km)

    Transfer to hotel

    Dinner in local restaurant

    Overnight in Samarkand

Transfer to city and shopping in famous dry fruit market “Siyab” – here you can find more than 100 types f dry fruits , souvenirs, hand-made pottery products and etc.

    Optionally visit to wine showroom and wine tasting in factory Khovrenko or carpet factory to see the how to made oriental carpets.

    Take a high speed train “Afrosiyab” to Tashkent (300km/2hrs/10minutes).

    Arrival transfer to hotel.

    Dinner at local restaurant

    Overnight in Tashkent
